Bleeding

Apply pressure: Use a clean cloth or bandage to apply direct pressure to the wound. If possible, elevate the affected limb above the level of the heart to slow down the bleeding.....

What to do is this situation

  • Get someone to call emergency.
  • Open the airway.
  • With the person lying on his or her back, tilt the head back slightly to lift the chin.
  • Listen carefully for sounds of breathing, for no more 10 seconds (occasional gasping sounds are not the same as breathing).
  • If there is no breathing, begin CPR.
  • Start chest compression immediately. Using both hands, push down hard and fast in center of the chest, allowing the chest to come back up naturally between compression. Continue until someone with more training has arrived.

What to do is this situation

  • Wash your hands or put on disposable gloves if possible. This can help protect you from diseases
  • Rinse the wound with water.
  • Cover the wound with a gauze or cloth (towel, blanket, clothing, whatever’s available).
  • Apply direct pressure to stop the blood flow and encourage clothing which is when blood naturally thickens to stop blood loss.
  • If possible, elevate the bleeding body part above heart.
  • If the cloth or bandage becomes soaked with blood, do not remove it, but add another layer on top of it and continue to apply pressure.
  • Once bleeding has stopped, apply a clean bandage.

What to do is this situation

  • Call for emergency medical help immediately, especially if the bone is protruding through the skin or if the injury involves the head, neck, or spine.
  • While waiting for medical help, keep the injured area immobile by using a splint or a makeshift splint. You can create a splint by using anything rigid like a stick or a rolled-up newspaper and securing it in place with a bandage, cloth, or tape.
  • Apply ice to the injured area to help reduce swelling and pain. Wrap the ice in a towel or cloth to avoid direct contact with the skin.
  • If the person is in pain, provide over-the-counter pain relief medication as directed on the packaging.
  • Keep the person still and calm until medical help arrives.
It's important to remember that you should never try to set a broken bone yourself, as this can cause further damage or complications. Instead, wait for medical professionals to provide appropriate care.

What to do is this situation

The first thing to do is to call emergency services or have someone else do it. While waiting for help, follow these steps:
  • Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.
  • Make a fist with one hand and place it just above the navel, but below the ribcage.
  • Grasp your fist with your other hand and thrust upward quickly and forcefully, as if trying to lift the person up and out of their chair.
  • Repeat the thrusts until the object is dislodged or the person becomes unconscious.
  • If the person loses consciousness, gently lower them to the ground and begin CPR, starting with chest compression.
  • If the person is conscious and able to cough or speak, encourage them to keep coughing to try and expel the object.
